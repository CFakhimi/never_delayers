  1. Description of the application

This tool will help airline passengers plan their travel by estimating the flight delay time in minutes based on historical data for their flight route. Passengers can enter flight information and see how long it is delayed, how often it is delayed and how likely they are to make their connection. They will also see how that flight compares to other flights on a variety of metrics like prices and delay time.

  1. Usefulness.

        There does not seem to be anything out there that does exactly what we are proposing. There are sites that show global delays in real time as well as some data about delays. They mainly focus on reporting delays that have happened as well as airports where you can expect delays in future flights in the next few hours. We are different because we look at historical data instead of immediate data which admittedly can be less accurate. We will also give users more information about how their flight specifically may be impacted and what they could do about it. Lastly, we may at some point integrate some of these real time sites data into our approach to improve accuracy.

  1. Realness.

As of right now we have found two promising look datasets on Kaggle. The first one is data about flight delays and the second one focuses on the prices of various flights. This will be the basis for the majority of our data, but it is possible that we could bring in more sources in the future. Admittedly, the prices dataset ends at the end of 2022 and the delays a few months ago, but we think they shall be sufficient to provide enough accuracy for our needs.

  1. Description of the functionality
  1. Basic Functions:

                Allow users to add in the details of their flight (info on delays). Only the user that inputs the data or an admin will be allowed to delete it from the database. Allow users to query average flight delay based on the Airline, Airport, and flight (SRC to DEST). These results will be printed and shown to the users. They will also be shown data on how their flight compares to other flights in the database. There is real flight data in the database.

  1. Advanced Functions:

                Finding alternative flights for less delay/less likely of a delay: We plan on calculating alternative routes to the same destination that are less likely to be delayed and are a similar price. While we would not necessarily provide users with an exact flight (although we could potentially do this) we can show them a different path to take to their destination using our database. Also, we could look at data between airlines and recommend a different airline instead. We will need to make a separate table that keeps track of which airlines operate out which airports.

Pulling in weather data to quantify delays associated with weather: We will try to pull in live weather data to create more scientific predictions on delays by correlating certain weather patterns with delay lengths. This could potentially work in a machine learning model that trains off of data of past delays and weather patterns to work on predicting delays with current weather patterns. As weather data is only available about a week in advance, this would be a somewhat time-gated feature.
